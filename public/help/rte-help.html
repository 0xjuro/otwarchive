Rich Text

Strikethrough and Underline

Pasting text with strikethrough or underline formatting into the Rich Text Editor (RTE) only works if it is copied from a source that uses strike, del, or u elements instead of inline styling. This means pasting from most text editing programs won't retain strikethrough or underline formatting, but pasting from some many pages will.

Google Drive

In addition to strikethrough and underline, text alignment and bold and italic formatting are also lost when pasting from Google Drive documents, and all elements have a dir attribute added. Pressing Enter between paragraphs to insert a blank line between paragraphs in Google Drive results in extra blank paragraphs. Structural formatting such as headings, lists, links, and tables is retained.

Pasting into the RTE from Google Drive also has a number of browser-specific quirks.

  • The iPhone does not copy any formatting from Google Drive documents.
  • Opera will often activate the bold option when pasting from Google Drive.
  • Firefox appears to preserve alignment; however, this is due to its use of the style attribute, which our sanitizer removes.

LibreOffice

Pressing Enter between paragraphs to insert a blank line between paragraphs in LibreOffice results in extra blank paragraphs. In tables, tr has a valign attribute set (it will be removed by our sanitizer) and th and td have the width attribute set.

Scrivener

Skipping a line between paragraphs (i.e. pressing Return twice) results in extra paragraph tags. In tables, td has a valign attribute set (it will be removed by our sanitizer). In addition to strikethrough and underline, center alignment and blockquotes are lost.

Using Scrivener's "Copy as HTML" option and pasting into the HTML editor tends to produce better results than copying and pasting into the RTE. While "Copy as HTML" includes many style attributes, they will be removed by our sanitizer.
